EndoMatrix Core

Personal endocrine pattern interpreter

A mobile-first web application that transforms daily symptom logs into interpretable hormonal patterns. Core includes a built-in cycle tracker — users log their period and cycle data directly in the app, or link their existing tracker of choice. Daily symptoms are then anchored to cycle phase, letting the system map pain, energy, and mood to specific hormonal windows over time. Over 30 days, the system surfaces cycle-phase patterns, escalation signals, and the "I finally see it" moment — building a structured record users can bring to clinical conversations.

  • Built-in cycle tracker — or link your existing period app
  • Daily 3-input log: pain, energy, dominant symptom — under 60 seconds
  • Symptoms anchored to cycle phase, not just calendar date
  • 30-day insight: when symptoms begin, how they escalate, what clusters together
  • Clinic-ready summaries users can share or export
  • Future: wearable device and hormone patch integrations in development

Why I built this

I have watched households navigate late-stage diabetes complications, the kind that arrive years after the warning signals first appeared and went unread. When I began talking to women experiencing severe menstrual symptoms, the pattern felt familiar. Real, persistent pain that the health system treats episodically, if at all.

Fifteen discovery interviews across urban Nairobi, rural Western Kenya, and Eastern Kenya confirmed this was structural. Women knew something was wrong. They had no tool to document it longitudinally, no record to bring to a clinician, and no way to interrupt the cycle of pain, relief, and postponed care.

EndoMatrix exists to give women that record, and to give health systems the anticipatory signals they need to act before complications become the only evidence anyone will listen to.

How It Works

From signals to anticipatory care

01
Sense

Women log a small set of daily signals — pain, energy, dominant symptom in under 60 seconds via app or SMS/USSD. Optional wearable proxies increase signal confidence where available.

02
Learn

The system learns each person's individual baseline — their endocrine fingerprint and detects meaningful drift from it, rather than comparing against population averages that erase normal variation.

03
Act

Interpretable pattern summaries help users anticipate difficult days and articulate their experience clinically. At scale, de-identified cohort signals enable health systems to intervene earlier.

Key research insight

Discovery interviews revealed a pain-relief-delay loop: during debilitating episodes, women plan to seek care — but once symptoms ease, life catches up and they postpone until the next cycle. This repeats indefinitely. EndoMatrix's low-burden, continuous logging is designed specifically to break this cycle, keeping the signal visible between episodes and building the longitudinal record women need for clinical conversations.
